Norse Pig

Norse Pig is a gift exchange game that draws on elements of luck to decide on who gets what.

Gift Value Requirement: $50

  • The paid value (before taxes) of the gift must be at least $50
    • Important: You cannot pay less than $50 for your gift. It's okay to pay more than $50 or find a deal for an item that's worth $70 and only pay $50, but you can't pay less than $50.
  • Must surrender receipt (proof of purchase) showing purchase information to be eligible to play.
  • You can combine multiple items to reach the value requirement. Just be sure to keep them together as they can't be broken up during the game.
  • The gift must be gender-neutral. Since the final recipient ultimately has no control over what he/she receives, let's be sure the gifts are not women's earrings or men's underwear. Bring something you think anyone would be happy to have.
  • All are welcome to play (adults, kids, animals) as long as you bring a gift that meets the criteria. If you bring a gift, you can expect to receive a gift in return.
  • No digital media items (like movies, music, video games) and no DVDs or Blu-ray. Board games are allowed.

How to play

Setup

  • 3+ players.
  • Gifts wrapped in center of table.

Round 1: Rank Players

  • Deal all players one 5-card poker hand.
  • Assign numbers by hand strength: smallest number #1 (best hand) to biggest number (worst hand).
  • Ties: Redeal only tied players until resolved.
  • Player with smallest number picks/opens gift from center first, then next smallest, etc.

Round 2: Challenges

Gifts visible.

  • Turns proceed in order from smallest number to biggest among active (non-retired) players. On your turn, you may issue a challenge or pass.
  • You may challenge only players with bigger numbers. Smallest number can challenge anyone; biggest number can challenge none.
  • Resolve: New 5-card poker hand (challenger vs. target).
    • Challenger wins: Swap gifts. Numbers unchanged. Challenger retires (no further challenges).
    • Challenger loses: No gift swap. Swap numbers. Challenger stays active.
  • After a challenge, if any active players remain, continue with the next turn starting again from the current smallest number among active players.

End

  • Game ends when one active player remains (now holds biggest number).
  • All keep current gifts.

Scenario 1: The player who has the ranking "A" decides to pass. The player retires. Game moves on to the player who has the ranking "K".

Scenario 2: The player who has the ranking "A" decides to challenge the player who has the ranking "J".

  • If "A" wins, the two players swap gifts. "A" retires. Game moves on to the player who has the ranking "K".
  • If "J" wins, no gifts are swapped. "A" takes over the ranking "J", and "J" takes over the ranking "A". Both players remain active. The new "A" takes an action of passing or challenging.

When there is only one active player left, the game ends and everybody keeps their current gifts.
